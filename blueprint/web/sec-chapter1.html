Definition 1.1 Complete metric space
#

Lean declarations

  • RP.CM_Complete

Let \((X,\mathbf d)\) be a metric space. We say \(X\) is complete if every Cauchy sequence converges in \(X\).

Definition 1.2 Contraction
#

Lean declarations

  • RP.CM_Contraction

Let \((X,\mathbf d)\) be a metric space and let \(\kappa \in [0,1)\). A map \(T:X\to X\) is a contraction with constant \(\kappa \) if for all \(x,y\in X\),

\[ \mathbf d(Tx,Ty)\le \kappa \, \mathbf d(x,y). \]
Definition 1.3 Picard iterates
#

Lean declarations

Theorem 1.4 Contraction Mapping—existence and uniqueness
#

Lean declarations

  • ContractingWith.exists_fixedPoint

Let \((X,\mathbf d)\) be complete and \(T:X\to X\) a contraction with constant \(\kappa {\lt}1\). Then there exists a unique fixed point \(\tilde x\in X\) with \(T(\tilde x)=\tilde x\).

Theorem 1.5 Convergence and geometric rate
#

Uses

  • Theorem 1.4

Lean declarations

  • ContractingWith.apriori_edist_iterate_efixedPoint_le

Under the same hypotheses, for any \(x\in X\) and all \(n\ge 0\),

\[ \mathbf d\! \big(T^{\, n}(x),\tilde x\big) \; \le \; \frac{\kappa ^{\, n}}{1-\kappa }\, \mathbf d\! \big(T(x),x\big), \]

hence \(T^{\, n}(x)\to \tilde x\) as \(n\to \infty \).

Theorem 1.17 Radii polynomials in finite dimensions
#

Uses

  • Definition 1.13
  • Lemma 1.14
  • Lemma 1.15
  • Lemma 1.16

Lean declarations

  • RP.RadiiPolynomialFiniteDim

If there exists \(r_0{\gt}0\) with \(p(r_0){\lt}0\), then:

  1. \(T\) maps \(B_{r_0}(\bar x)\) into itself and is a contraction there;

  2. there exists a unique \(\tilde x\in B_{r_0}(\bar x)\) with \(T(\tilde x)=\tilde x\);

  3. \(f(\tilde x)=0\), and \(\| I-ADf(\tilde x)\| {\lt}1\), hence \(Df(\tilde x)\) is invertible.

Proof ▶

From \(p(r_0){\lt}0\) we get \(Z(r_0)=Z_0+Z_2(r_0)r_0{\lt}1\) and \((Z(r_0)-1)r_0+Y_0{\lt}0\). Apply Lemma 1.15 with \(r=r_0\) to obtain (i) and (ii); the existence and uniqueness follow from Theorem ??. Then \(T(\tilde x)=\tilde x\) implies \(A f(\tilde x)=0\), hence \(f(\tilde x)=0\) (since \(A\) is injective); finally \(\| I-ADf(\tilde x)\| {\lt}1\) implies \(Df(\tilde x)\) is invertible by Lemma 1.16.
