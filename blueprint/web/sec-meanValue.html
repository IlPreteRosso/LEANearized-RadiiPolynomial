Suppose that

\(\) U ⊂R^n\( is open and that \)f: U →R^n\( is \) C^1\(. Consider \) x\(, y \in U\) such that the line segment

\begin{equation*} (1-t)x + ty, \quad t \in [0,1] \end{equation*}

is contained in \( U\). Then\(, \begin{equation*} f(y) - f(x) = \left(\int _0^1 \text{Df}((1-t)x + ty) \, dt\right)(y-x), \end{equation*} where \)Df\( denotes the Jacobian of \) f.

\begin{equation*} \| f(y) - f(x)\| _\infty \leq \left(\sup _{z \in \overline{B}_\rho (x_0)} \| \text{Df}(z)\| _\infty \right) \| y-x\| _\infty , \end{equation*}
