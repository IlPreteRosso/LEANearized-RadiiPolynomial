Theorem 0.1.3 Mean Value Theorem
#

Lean declarations

  • RP.MeanValueTheorem

Let \(g:\mathbb R^n\to \mathbb R\) be \(C^1\). For all \(x,y\in \mathbb R^n\) there exists \(t\in (0,1)\) such that

\[ g(x)-g(y)=\nabla g\bigl(y+t(x-y)\bigr)\cdot (x-y). \]

Theorem 0.1.5 Contraction Mapping Theorem
#

Lean declarations

  • RP.ContractionMapping

Let \((X,d)\) be complete and \(T:X\to X\) be \(L\)-Lipschitz with \(L{\lt}1\). Then \(T\) has a unique fixed point \(x^\ast \in X\), and the iterates \(x_{k+1}=T(x_k)\) converge to \(x^\ast \) for every \(x_0\in X\).

Lemma 0.1.11 Fixed-point radii
#

Uses

  • Lemma 0.1.4
  • Lemma 0.1.10
  • Definition 0.1.1
  • Theorem 0.1.5

Lean declarations

  • RP.FixedPointRadii

Let \(r{\gt}0\). Assume \(\| T(\bar x)-\bar x\| \le Y_0\) and \(\sup _{c\in B_r(\bar x)}\| DT(c)\| \le Z(r)\). If \((Z(r)-1)r+Y_0{\lt}0\), then \(T(B_r(\bar x))\subseteq B_r(\bar x)\) and \(T\) is a contraction on \(B_r(\bar x)\) with Lipschitz constant \(\le Z(r){\lt}1\).

Lemma 0.1.12 Neumann-series invertibility
#

Lean declarations

  • RP.NeumannInvertible

If \(\| I-B\| {\lt}1\) for a matrix \(B\), then \(B\) is invertible (and \(B^{-1}=\sum _{k\ge 0}(I-B)^k\)).

Theorem 0.1.13 Radii polynomials in finite dimensions
#

Uses

  • Definition 0.1.9
  • Lemma 0.1.10
  • Lemma 0.1.11
  • Lemma 0.1.12
  • Theorem 0.1.5

Lean declarations

  • RP.RadiiPolynomialFiniteDim

If there exists \(r_0{\gt}0\) with \(p(r_0){\lt}0\), then:

  1. \(T\) maps \(B_{r_0}(\bar x)\) into itself and is a contraction there;

  2. there exists a unique \(\tilde x\in B_{r_0}(\bar x)\) with \(T(\tilde x)=\tilde x\);

  3. \(f(\tilde x)=0\), and \(\| I-ADf(\tilde x)\| {\lt}1\), hence \(Df(\tilde x)\) is invertible.

Proof ▶

From \(p(r_0){\lt}0\) we get \(Z(r_0)=Z_0+Z_2(r_0)r_0{\lt}1\) and \((Z(r_0)-1)r_0+Y_0{\lt}0\). Apply Lemma 0.1.11 with \(r=r_0\) to obtain (i) and (ii); the existence and uniqueness follow from Theorem 0.1.5. Then \(T(\tilde x)=\tilde x\) implies \(A f(\tilde x)=0\), hence \(f(\tilde x)=0\) (since \(A\) is injective); finally \(\| I-ADf(\tilde x)\| {\lt}1\) implies \(Df(\tilde x)\) is invertible by Lemma 0.1.12.
